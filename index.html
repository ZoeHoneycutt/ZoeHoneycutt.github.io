    Interests

    The earliest from of 'books' were clay tablets with cuneiform writing created by the Sumerians in Mesopotamia around 3500 BC, with Papyrus emerging in Egypt around 3000 BC. Ancient Egyptians pressed and used Papyrus reeds to create scrolls. Woodblock printing, which was made by China in the 2nd-5th century CE, including carving characters onto wooden blocks to press ink into paper. which was a big step towards mass production books. The Diamond Sutra (868 CE) Was the world's earliest production book, produced using woodblock printing in China. The Mechanical printing press was made out of metal movable type revolutionized book production that lead to Mass production books like the Bible. The prining press was made by Johannes Gutenberg in Europe in 1455, who also printed the Gutenberg Bible. True paper was created in China (A Chinese court official) by Cai Lun around 105 CE, made from mullberry bark, hemp, rags, and fishing nets. In other parts of the world animal skins were used to create albeit less convenient, writing materials. Parchment paper and Vellum more durable material created around 6th century BCE that was created out of animal skin that could be written on both sides. Books became more modernized around the late 20th, Early 21st Century after the inventions of Steam-Powered presses and Typewriters that made is easier to Produces Mass quantities of books. The first Ebook was created by Michael Hart in 1971 by manually typing the Declaration of Independence into a computer and distributing it over a network as project Gutenberg, a free digital library. After that more ideas, websites, and articles were released, and eventually the Amazon Kindle was invented which started the EBook Era.
